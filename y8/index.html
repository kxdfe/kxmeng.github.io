Shapes and solids

  1. Know the definition of a prism and specific names
  2. Use nets as 2D representations of 3D solids
  3. Calculate the surface area and volume of prisms
  4. Use appropriate language and terminology for the parts of a circle
  5. Understand the significance of \(\pi\) and be able to use it on a calculator
  6. Calculate and solve problems involving the circumference and area of a circle, as well as related composite shapes (semi circles, qudrants and annuli, not sectors or segments)
  7. Apply knowledge of prisms and circles to cylinders
  8. Use Pythagoras' Theorem to find a missing length using a calculator and rounding to a given degree of accuracy
  9. Knowledge of Pythagorean triples
  10. (Extension: Geometrical proof of Pythagoras' Theorem)
  11. (Extension: Application of the theorem to real life problems)

Transformations

  1. Describe and carry out translations (including with vectors), reflections (equations for mirror lines), and rotations. Tracing paper may be used
  2. Describe and carry out enlargements using positive integer, negative and fractional scale factors
  3. Transform 2D shapes using a combination of transformations

Fractions, decimals and percentages

  1. Revise fractions, decimals and percentages
  2. Use multipliers to calculate percentage changes
  3. Work out an original quantity before a percentage change

Constructions and loci

  1. Use a pair of compasses to accurately draw circles
  2. Construct triangles accurately using rulers and compasses
  3. Draw diagrams to scale
  4. Construction of perpendicular and angle bisectors
  5. Draw a locus and use loci to solve problems

Probability

  1. Use appropriate probability language (likely, certain, impossible, even chance, unlikely) and the 0 - 1 probability scale
  2. Identify mutually exclusive events
  3. Use probability notation and appreciate that \(p(A') = 1 - p(A)\)
  4. Calculate relative frequency and use it to make estimates of the probability of an event and to calculate expected frequencies
  5. Record, describe and analyse the frequency of outcomes of simple probability experiments involving randomness, fairness, equally and unequally likely outcomes. Work out expected results when an experiment is repeated
  6. Generate theoretical sample spaces (e.g. lists of outcomes or tables) for single and combined events and use these to calculate theoretical probabilities
  7. Use tree diagrams to find the probability of two or more events
  8. Extension: Probability with Venn Diagrams

Graphs

  1. Plot straight line graphs from tables
  2. Find the y-intercept of a straight line
  3. Find the gradient of a straight line
  4. Plot graphs using the y-intercept and gradient
  5. Use \(y=mx+c\) and find the equation of a line from its graph
  6. Understand the relationship between parallel and perpendicular lines.

Speed, distance and time

  1. Revise direct proportion
  2. Draw and interpret distance time graphs
  3. Use the graphs to solve problems
  4. Calculate average speed from distance and time (relate to gradient)

Scale drawings and measures

  1. Interpret scale drawings and use a scale in converting between real and drawn distances e.g. 1:100,000 is equivalent to 1cm to 1km.
  2. Relate scales to ratio
  3. Revise angle rules in parallel lines
  4. Bearings measured in degrees, clockwise from the North. Calculate back bearings
  5. The link between bearings and eight point compass directions
  6. Combining up to three stages of a journey, given in terms of distances and bearings
  7. Similarity and congruence

Powers and roots

  1. Revise fractions
  2. Find the reciprocal of a number and work with reciprocals
  3. Revise indices rules
  4. Work with negative indices and work out powers of fractions
  5. Write and order numbers in standard form (positive and negative powers)
  6. Calculate with numbers in standard from

Inequalities, equations and formulae

  1. Solve linear inequalities and represent the solution on a number line
  2. Use indices laws with algebraic terms, including zero and negative powers
  3. Revise solving linear equations
  4. Solve linear equations involving algebraic fractions
  5. Change the subject of a basic formula
  6. Change algebraic fractions in equivalent fractions, finding an algebraic common denominator
  7. Solve more complex problems with fractions in formulae

Graphical solutions

  1. Solve simple simultaneous equations by substitution and elimination
  2. Solve more complex simultaneous equations
  3. Revise \(y=mx+c\) and rearrange equations to find the gradient and y-intercept
  4. Find an equation of a line between two points
  5. Solve simultaneous equations by drawing graphs

Quadratic graphs

  1. Understand the parabolic shape of quadratic functions and identify their graphs
  2. Draw quadratic graphs from tables of values

Proof

  1. Explain, show and justify a mathematical solution
  2. Identify the difference between giving an example and proving a theory
  3. Understand how to use mathematical proof
  4. Present a logical argument using algebra
